## Core Finding

268 DHARMA prasasti scanned for material-culture keywords:

| Class | Inscriptions | % of Corpus |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Organic** (kayu, bambu, atap, ijuk, daun, rotan, jati) | **170** | **63.4%** |
| Lithic (batu, bata, candi, prasada, mandapa, stambha) | 73 | 27.2% |
| Metal (emas, perak, tembaga, besi, timah) | 148 | 55.2% |

- **103 organic-only** inscriptions vs **6 lithic-only** (binomial p < 0.0001)
- Paired comparison: organic variety > lithic in 43/44 non-tied cases

**The people who carved stone inscriptions documented a world built of wood, bamboo, and thatch.**

## Genre Analysis (E040c) — Meta-Taphonomic

C8 inscriptions (n=55) show only 9% organic mentions. Why?

- C8 = **93% Sanskrit**, 2% sima format, median 1,364 characters
- C9-C11 = 65-100% Old Javanese, 55-64% sima, median 5,464 characters
- **Sima genre predicts organic mentions: 84.7% vs 33.6%** (OR = 10.96, p < 0.000001)

The organic economy was always there. C8 inscriptions were short Sanskrit dedications — the genre made organic materials invisible. When sima format standardized in C9, organic world appears at 68-91%.

**This is P1's argument applied recursively: what gets preserved depends on what gets recorded, and what gets recorded depends on the genre of inscription.**

## Usage in P1 Revision Response

If reviewer says: *"How do you know the archaeological gap isn't simply absence of settlement?"*

Response: "The epigraphic record itself documents a predominantly organic material culture. Of 268 surviving inscriptions, 170 (63.4%) mention organic building materials (wood, bamboo, thatch, palm fiber) while only 73 (27.2%) mention lithic materials (stone, brick). The archaeological 'blank' is not an absence of civilization — it is a preservation bias. The people who carved these stone inscriptions lived in wooden houses."

If reviewer says: *"This could be a genre artifact of sima inscriptions."*

Response: "Precisely — and this strengthens our argument. Sima (land grant) inscriptions document the real economy, while Sanskrit dedications record religious ideology. When the recording genre shifts from Sanskrit dedications (C8) to Old Javanese administrative documents (C9+), organic material culture suddenly becomes visible — not because the economy changed, but because the medium of record expanded. Taphonomic bias operates on genre, not just physical preservation."
