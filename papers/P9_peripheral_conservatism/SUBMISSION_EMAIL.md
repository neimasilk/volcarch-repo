**Title:** Peripheral Conservatism as Archaeological Proxy: Linguistic, Ritual, and Botanical Evidence for a Pre-Hindu Nusantaran Substrate

**Authors:** Mukhlis Amien (corresponding) and Go Frendi Gunawan, Lab Data Sains, Universitas Bhinneka Nusantara, Indonesia

**Word count:** Approximately 10,000 words (including footnotes)

**Summary:** The paper proposes the Peripheral Conservatism Framework (PCF), a systematic methodology for recovering pre-Hindu cultural substrates in Nusantara from living peripheral communities rather than from archaeological excavation at overwritten centres. Three independent evidence channels — lexical conservation (ABVD cognacy analysis), mortuary ritual structure (Trunyan, Toraja, famadihana), and botanical burial practice (the Plumeria/Canarium substitution chain) — converge on a consistent pattern: peripheral communities retain archaic Austronesian elements that centres have lost through successive overwriting by Indianization, Islamization, and colonialism. Madagascar provides a dateable c. 1200 CE calibration point.
