Link text: Figure 1
Legend:
Dwarapala guardian statue at Singosari, East Java. Left: photographed ~1860 when half-buried (~185 cm) by volcanic sediment (source: Leiden University Library). Right: present-day view after excavation, with person for scale; total statue height ~370 cm. The burial of half the statue over ~510 years yields a sedimentation rate of 3.6 mm/yr.


FIGURE 2
--------
File: fig2_dwarapala_timeline.tif
Link text: Figure 2
Legend:
Empirical calibration of volcanic sedimentation rate from the Dwarapala Singosari. Timeline from construction (~1268 CE) through discovery (1803 CE, ~185 cm buried) to present (~265 cm buried). Rate = 185 cm / 510 yr = 3.6 mm/yr, consistent with cross-system mean of 4.4 ± 1.2 mm/yr from multiple calibration points.

Manuscript Type: Project Gallery
Title: Spatial segregation of deep-time archaeological sites from volcanic plains in East Java: evidence for taphonomic burial
Abstract: All four known pre-Neolithic Java sites occupy karst caves or river terraces far from volcanic centres, despite high-suitability volcanic plains nearby. Spatial analysis of 65,432 grid cells shows a monotonic gradient: deeper tephra burial correlates with fewer surface sites (Cohen's d = 1.005). This taphonomic bias may mask deep-time occupation across volcanically active regions.
Keywords: volcanic taphonomy; archaeological site distribution; East Java; settlement suitability model; tephra burial; Southeast Asia
