# E025: Quantitative Validation of the Volcanic Ritual Clock Hypothesis

## Hypothesis
The correspondence between slametan intervals (3, 7, 40, 100, 1000 days) and forensic decomposition stages in tropical volcanic soil is non-random and robust to reasonable parameter variation.

## Sub-experiments

### 1. Monte Carlo Interval Matching
**Question:** What is the probability that 5 randomly chosen intervals would match 5 independently-defined decomposition stages as well as the slametan intervals do?
**Method:** Permutation test + Monte Carlo simulation (uniform and log-uniform sampling)
**Addresses:** "Pattern fitting / just-so story" criticism

### 2. Grave Subsidence Model
**Question:** Can decomposition stages be observed from the surface of a shallow (~75cm) tropical grave?
**Method:** Computational model of body volume loss → soil settlement timing
**Addresses:** "Observation mechanism is weak — Javanese bury, they don't exhume" criticism

### 3. ADD Decomposition Model
**Question:** What does a parameterized Accumulated Degree-Day model predict for each slametan interval in Javanese conditions?
**Method:** ADD calculation with literature-derived stage boundaries, sensitivity analysis across temperature and pH ranges
**Addresses:** "No empirical data for Javanese andosol" criticism (provides computational prediction)

## Data
- Forensic decomposition literature (Rodriguez & Bass 1985; Galloway 1989; Megyesi 2005; Haglund & Sorg 1997; Nielsen-Marsh & Hedges 2000)
- Slametan intervals from ethnographic sources (Geertz 1960; Hendrajaya & Almu'tasim 2020)
- Environmental parameters from ISRIC SoilGrids and climate data

## Results

### Monte Carlo: p < 0.001 (uniform), p = 0.009 (log-uniform narrow)
- Slametan matches 5/5 decomposition stages
- Only 11/500,000 random interval sets achieve this (uniform)
- No other mortuary tradition achieves 5/5 match (Toraja = 4/5, Orthodox = 4/4)
- Cross-tradition comparison strengthens uniqueness claim

### Grave Subsidence: Observation mechanism is physically plausible
- Odor peaks at day 7 (95% detectable through 75cm soil)
- Ground subsidence visible (~3cm) by day 40
- Surface signals sufficient for empirical knowledge accumulation
- No exhumation required
