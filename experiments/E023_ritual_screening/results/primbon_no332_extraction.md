## No. 327 — Tanda untuk orang akan meninggal dunia (hal. 242–243)

Signs (sasmita) visible to the dying person:
1. Sering merasa letih, bosan, mimpi pergi ke Utara → kurang 3 tahun
2. Rindu kepada yang sudah meninggal, mimpi memperbaiki rumah → kurang 2 tahun
3. Sering melihat yang tidak nampak → kurang 1 tahun
4. Sering melihat sifat matanya sendiri → kurang 9 bulan
5. Sering mendengar suara jin/syaitan/hewan → kurang 6 bulan
6. Sering mencium bau mahluk halus (kemenyan terbakar, bau amis) → kurang 3 bulan
7. Berganti penglihatannya, air berwarna merah → kurang 2 bulan
8. **Jari telunjuk dan jari manis ditekuk ke telapak tangan, jarimanis dapat diangkat → kurang 40 hari**
9. Melihat tangan/pergelangan tangan terlihat sudah putus → kurang 1 bulan
10. Sering melihat wajahnya sendiri → kurang setengah bulan (15 hari)
11. Enggan berbuat sesuatu, tidak suka makan/tidur → kurang 1 minggu
12. Merasa gerah-uyang, mengeluarkan tinja kalong → kurang 3 hari
13. Semua babahan hawasanga terasa mengeluarkan angin → kurang 2 hari
14. Otot pergelangan kaki kendor, keringat seperti lelah → kurang 1 hari
15. Kulitnya disentuh tidak terasa, telinga tidak berbunyi → saatnya meninggal

Signs visible to OTHERS:
1. Selalu dimanjakan (linulu) → kurang 3 tahun
2. Perbedaan gerak dari kebiasaan → kurang 2 tahun
3. Gerakannya serba baru → kurang 1 tahun
4. Tabiat berubah (bengis → lembut, suka keramaian → menyepi) → kurang 6 bulan
5. Berwatak seperti anak-anak → kurang 4 bulan
6. Sinar matanya pudar → kurang 1 bulan
7. Sinar mata telah condong → kurang 1 minggu
8. Roman muka pucat, hidung kempis, badan dingin, bau tubuhnya sawa → mendekati ajal

**SIGNIFICANCE FOR P5:** Entry 8 in self-observed signs explicitly uses the 40-day interval as a prognostic marker. This places the number 40 in a pre-death predictive context within the Primbon itself, reinforcing that 40 days was a recognized temporal unit associated with bodily change.

## No. 331 — Jika Jenasah berbau busuk (hal. 244)

"If the corpse smells bad" — a mantra/prayer to recite:

> "Sir suci mulya sejati, banyu urip kang winasuhan, sumberan, idayatolah, sir ganda raga arum hura heri raga muliha karsa, karsa awor lan suksma, suksma ningsun sakuduping mlati, les angles ing lautan, suksma larah suksama larih sukma mulya, raga tan kena kari, hu dat les, hu dat les."

**SIGNIFICANCE FOR P5:** This entry — placed immediately before the selamatan calculation table (No. 332) — demonstrates that the Primbon authors were aware of and explicitly addressed corpse decomposition. The juxtaposition of a decomposition-related entry with the selamatan calendar is not coincidental: it reflects an indigenous framework in which ritual timing and bodily decay are conceptually linked.

## No. 332 — Selamatan orang meninggal (hal. 245–246) ★ KEY ENTRY

### The Selamatan Calculation Table

A full-page table mapping the day of death (Nyur tanah / Surtanah) to the hari and pasaran for each subsequent selamatan:

| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Nyur tanah | Day of death (hari + pasaran) |
| 3 Hari | Nelung dina (3rd day) |
| 7 Hari | Mitung dina (7th day) |
| 40 Hari | Matang puluh (40th day) |
| 100 Hari | Nyatus (100th day) |
| Mendhak | Mendhak (1st anniversary) |
| Nyewu | Nyewu (1000th day) |

### Example (from text, hal. 246):

If someone dies on **Kamis Wage**, the selamatan schedule is:
- 3 hari → **Sabtu Legi**
- 7 hari → **Rabu Kliwon**
- 40 hari → **Senin Pon**
- 100 hari → **Jumat Pon**
- Mendhak → **Ahad Paing**
- Nyewu → **Selasa Pon**

### Key definitions (verbatim):

> "Nyurtanah (Surtanah) ialah hari ketika si X meninggal dunia."
> (Nyurtanah is the day when person X passes away.)

> **"Nyewu ialah selamatan hari keseribu terhitung dari meninggal dunianya si mati."**
> (Nyewu is the selamatan on the thousandth day counted from the death of the deceased.)

### Abbreviations:
- Jm = Jum'at, St = Sabtu, Ah = Ahad (Minggu), Sn = Senin, Sl = Selasa, Rb = Rabu, Km = Kamis
- Kl = Kliwon, Lg = Legi, Pa = Paing, Pn = Pon, Wg = Wage

**SIGNIFICANCE FOR P5:** This is a PRIMARY SOURCE documenting the complete slametan death sequence (3-7-40-100-Mendhak-Nyewu) in a traditional Javanese reference work. The Primbon treats the sequence as a practical calendrical calculation — a given input (day of death) produces a fixed output (schedule of ceremonies). This computational approach implies that the intervals were considered invariant and foundational, not arbitrary or negotiable. The explicit definition of Nyewu as "hari keseribu" (thousandth day) confirms this is exactly 1000 days, not a metaphorical or approximate figure.

## No. 333 — Merawat mayat (hal. 246–247)

Detailed instructions for preparing the corpse:

1. **Closing the body:** Eyes closed, jaw tied with cloth (sapu tangan), hands placed on stomach (right over left), legs straightened
2. **Washing:** Prayer recited: "Nawate ada'a farhelil gasli hadlal mayit." Washed with wudlu water, dried with handuk
3. **Wrapping (kafan):**
   - Cotton soaked with sandalwood paste (bubuk cendana) applied to ALL body openings: **mata, hidung, telinga, bibir (sampai menutup mulut), pusar, dubur, kemaluan, dan setiap persendian anggota badan**
   - Body wrapped in white cotton shroud (mori mentah), "dipocong"
   - Placed on funeral bier (peti mati/bandosa)
4. **Funeral prayer:** 10-step sholat jenazah described
5. **Burial:**
   - Body oriented pointing north (membujur ke utara), face slightly west (menghadap bawah/ke arah kiblat)
   - Covered with stone/wood/bamboo (batu, papan, bambu/glogor) at ~3/4 meter above to prevent soil collapse
   - Soil thrown in 2 times with prayers: first "minha chalakhakum", second "wafihna nui 'idukun"
   - Grave closed, prayer (ditelkim) recited

**SIGNIFICANCE FOR P5:** The detailed inventory of body openings and joints to be treated confirms that Javanese mortuary practice involved intimate knowledge of human anatomy. The list (eyes, nose, ears, mouth, navel, anus, genitals, joints) parallels the tissue inventory in the 40-day kasampurnan belief documented by Hendrajaya & Almu'tasim (2020). The specification of shallow burial depth (~3/4 meter) with a protective cover (glogor) but direct contact with earth is consistent with conditions promoting the decomposition timeline described in our taphonomic calibration.

## No. 334 — Berziarah ke kubur (hal. 247)

**Timing:** Bulan Ruwah, tanggal 15–30

**Entry prayer (Javanese):**
> "Bismillahirrochmanirrochim, alalamu alaikum, biyen ana ngembananne ratu, biyen eling saiki lali, elinge maring awakku, dadi teguh rahayu slamet, salalahu ngalaihi wassalam."

**At the gravestone:**
- Sit and hold incense (dupa), place in the perapian (burner)
- Javanese address to ancestors: "Sang galinggangjati araning menyan, urubing menyan, winurjati urubing menyan, dakjaluk gawemu, minangka bektiku marang bapa biyungku (parents), kaki-niniku (grandparents), lan leluhurku (ancestors)..."
- Recite: fatekah 3×, kulhu 3×, selawat 3×
- Place flowers on the nisan (gravestone): **mawar, melati, kenanga, kantil, selasih**
- During Ruwah 15–30: make selamatan with **ketan, kolak, and apem** distributed to saudara dan tetangga

**SIGNIFICANCE FOR P5:** The grave visitation practice during bulan Ruwah (the month before Ramadan) demonstrates ongoing engagement with the burial site. This regular visitation creates opportunities for observing the state of the grave and, by extension, the decomposition of the buried remains — precisely the mechanism through which empirical observation of decomposition could have been accumulated across generations.

## Summary: What the Primbon Tells Us

1. **The 3-7-40-100-Mendhak-Nyewu sequence is codified** as a fixed calculation in the Primbon, treated as practical knowledge (ilmu) rather than theological doctrine
2. **Nyewu is explicitly defined as the 1000th day** — not an approximate or metaphorical figure
3. **Decomposition awareness** is directly present (No. 331, placed immediately before the selamatan table)
4. **Anatomical knowledge** of the body is detailed in mortuary preparation (No. 333)
5. **The 40-day interval appears in multiple contexts** — both as a selamatan marker AND as a prognostic sign for approaching death (No. 327, sign #8)
6. **Grave visitation is institutionalized** (No. 334), creating the conditions for observational knowledge of decomposition
7. **The burial method** (shallow, with protective cover but earth contact) is consistent with the decomposition timeline proposed in P5
