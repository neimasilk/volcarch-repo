# Cover Letter — Oceanic Linguistics

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**To:** Editor, *Oceanic Linguistics*
University of Hawai'i Press

**Re:** Manuscript submission — "Phonological Fossils: Machine Learning Detection of Non-Mainstream Vocabulary in Sulawesi Basic Lexicon"

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit the enclosed manuscript for consideration as an article in *Oceanic Linguistics*.

This study introduces a machine learning methodology for detecting phonologically non-conforming vocabulary in Sulawesi Austronesian basic lexicon. Using 1,357 lexical forms from six Sulawesi languages in the Austronesian Basic Vocabulary Database, we combine a rule-based cognate subtraction method with an XGBoost classifier trained exclusively on phonological features. The classifier distinguishes inherited Austronesian vocabulary from non-mainstream forms with AUC = 0.763 after ablation of confound features, revealing a "phonological fingerprint" that generalizes across all six languages in leave-one-out testing.

The paper's most consequential finding is negative: phonological clustering of 266 high-confidence non-mainstream candidates yields no coherent cross-linguistic word families (p = 0.569), providing no support for the hypothesis that these forms derive from a single pre-Austronesian substrate language. The non-mainstream vocabulary is consistent with parallel independent innovations rather than shared substrate inheritance.

The manuscript also presents convergent evidence from the Hanacaraka script reduction, robustness validation through IPA conversion and syllable-count tests, and geographic expansion to 16 additional languages showing that Sulawesi languages have significantly higher predicted non-mainstream rates than Western Indonesian languages.

This manuscript has not been published previously and is not under consideration elsewhere. All data are from the publicly available Austronesian Basic Vocabulary Database. Analysis scripts are available at GitHub (https://github.com/neimasilk/volcarch-repo).

**AI Disclosure:** This research employed an AI-augmented workflow using Claude (Anthropic) for statistical scripting, cross-database comparison, and iterative experimental design. All research hypotheses, domain interpretation, and final scholarly judgments were made by the human authors. Full details are provided in the manuscript's AI Disclosure statement.

We confirm that this manuscript complies with the journal's ethical guidelines and represents original work.
