# Slametan Interval ↔ Decomposition Stage Mapping

## Purpose
Maps each Javanese slametan death ritual interval to the corresponding forensic decomposition stage in tropical volcanic soil (pH 4.5–5.5, T 25–28°C, high moisture). This table is a core component of Paper 5 Section 5.

## Key Environmental Variables

### Volcanic Soil (Andosol) Characteristics — Java
- **pH:** 4.5–5.5 (highly acidic due to tephra weathering)
- **Temperature:** 25–28°C annual average (tropical lowlands)
- **Moisture:** High (>2000mm annual rainfall, poor drainage in alluvial plains)
- **Microbial activity:** Very high (warm + wet + organic-rich)
- **Mineral composition:** Amorphous aluminum/iron compounds (allophane, imogolite)
- **Effect on bone:** Hydroxyapatite (Ca₁₀(PO₄)₆(OH)₂) dissolves below pH 6.0; rate increases sharply below pH 5.5

### Comparison: Limestone/Karst Soil
- **pH:** 7.0–8.5 (alkaline)
- **Effect on bone:** Hydroxyapatite STABLE above pH 6.0; bone can persist for millennia
- **Archaeological implication:** This is why Java's CAVE sites (limestone = high pH) preserve bone while OPEN-AIR volcanic plain sites do not

## The pH–Bone Preservation Gradient

```
pH 4.0-4.5  |████████████████████| Rapid dissolution — bone gone in <2 years
pH 4.5-5.5  |████████████████    | Fast dissolution — bone degraded in 2-5 years  ← JAVA VOLCANIC SOIL
pH 5.5-6.0  |████████████        | Moderate dissolution — bone survives 5-20 years
pH 6.0-7.0  |████████            | Slow dissolution — bone survives decades-centuries
pH 7.0-8.0  |████                | Minimal dissolution — bone survives millennia  ← JAVA CAVE SITES
pH 8.0-8.5  |██                  | Very stable — bone can survive >10,000 years
```

## Critical Insight: Why Slametan Timing Matches Volcanic Soil

The slametan sequence would NOT make sense in limestone environments:
- In limestone soil (pH 7+): bone persists for centuries → 1000-day "dust" claim would be observably FALSE
- In volcanic soil (pH 4.5–5.5): bone genuinely degrades within ~1000 days → claim is OBSERVABLY TRUE
- **The slametan was calibrated in the environment where it is taphonomically accurate**

This is the core of the "Volcanic Ritual Clock" argument: the ritual timing is not arbitrary mysticism — it reflects generations of empirical observation of what happens to buried bodies in acidic volcanic soil.

## Limitations / Caveats
1. Decomposition rates are highly variable (body mass, clothing, depth, season, scavenger access)
2. Most forensic studies are from temperate environments; tropical data is sparse
3. No published study has measured decomposition rates specifically in Javanese andosol
4. The mapping is APPROXIMATE — the claim is about ordering and rough magnitude, not exact timing
5. Burial depth matters: shallow burial (<1m) ≠ deep burial (>2m) in decomposition rate
6. Historical soil pH may differ from modern measurements (but andosol pH is geologically stable)
