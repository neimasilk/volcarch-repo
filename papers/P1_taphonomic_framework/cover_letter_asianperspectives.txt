Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit the enclosed manuscript, "Multi-Site Calibration of Volcanic Sedimentation Rates and Implications for Archaeological Visibility in Java, Indonesia," for consideration as a Research Article in Asian Perspectives.

This paper presents an empirical calibration framework for estimating archaeological burial depths in volcanic Java. Using four independent archaeological sites across two volcanic systems (Kelud and Merapi), we derive a mean cumulative sedimentation rate of 4.4 +/- 1.2 mm/yr over a 1,200-year horizon. We apply these rates to project that Kanjuruhan-era (A.D. ~760) remains in the Malang basin likely lie beneath 4-8 meters of overburden—exceeding the detection limits of surface survey and conventional ground-penetrating radar. The framework offers a quantitative baseline for directing future subsurface investigations in volcanic island arcs.

The manuscript is approximately 4,400 words (including references), contains 5 figures and 3 tables, and has not been published or submitted elsewhere.
