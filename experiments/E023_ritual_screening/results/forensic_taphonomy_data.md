## Key Reference Data

### Accumulated Degree Days (ADD) — Megyesi et al. 2005
- Formula: ADD = Σ(daily mean temp above 0°C)
- At 28°C tropical average: 1 calendar day = 28 ADD
- 1000 days = ~28,000 ADD — far beyond any soft tissue persistence
- TBS (Total Body Score) system: head + trunk + limbs, 1-13 point scale
- ADD accounts for ~80% of variation in decomposition rate

### Tropical Decomposition Rates
- **Townsville, tropical Queensland (Archer 2004):** Fresh Day 1 → active decay Day 3 → dry/remains Day 5 (fastest published)
- **Sri Lanka (retrospective):** Complete skeletonization 3 months to 1 year (surface). Earliest at 15 days (indoor).
- **Buried vs. surface:** Buried remains decompose ~8× slower (Carter & Tibbett 2008)
- **40 days at 28°C = 1,120 ADD** — correlates with advanced decay / early skeletonization in published studies

### Soil pH and Bone Preservation

#### Critical pH threshold: pH <5 promotes demineralization

**Mechanism:**
- Bone = ~70% hydroxyapatite Ca₁₀(PO₄)₆(OH)₂ + ~30% collagen
- Acidic soil dissolves mineral first → "jelly bone" (demineralized collagen) → biological degradation
- Alkaline soil degrades organic first → mineral persists (bone preserved for millennia)

**Experimental Data:**
- **Oghenemavwe et al. (2022):** Buried bone in pH 2.98 vs 7.10 vs 11.58 for 6 weeks. Measurable reduction in Haversian canal area in acidic soil after just 6 weeks (P ≤ 0.05).
- **Hedges & Millard (1995):** Dissolution rate proportional to [H+]^0.64 — strongly pH-dependent.
- **Star Carr (Milner et al. 2011, 2016):** pH 2–4 caused near-complete dissolution of bone hydroxyapatite, leaving only collagen.
- **Sutton Hoo:** Highly acidic sandy soil reduced bone to stained sand silhouettes.
- **Kemp (2016):** "High soil acidity associated with near complete mineral dissolution of recently buried human remains" — forensic case study.

#### Andosol (Javanese Volcanic Soil) Properties
- Classified as Andisols/Andosols
- pH decreases from East to West Java
- Characterized by allophane (amorphous clay), high organic matter
- pH 4.5–5.5 sits squarely in the zone where bone mineral dissolution is actively promoted

### Bone Weathering Stages (Behrensmeyer 1978)
| Stage | Description | Timeline (tropical) |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 0 | Unweathered, greasy | 0–1 years |
| 1 | Fine longitudinal cracking | 0–3 years |
| 2 | Flaking of outer layers | 2–6 years |
| 3 | Fibrous inner bone exposed | 4–15 years |
| 4 | Deep cracking, spalling | 6–15+ years |
| 5 | Fragile, unrecognizable | 6–15+ years |

In acidic soil: stages dramatically accelerated by chemical dissolution.

### Robert Hertz (1907) Connection
- Theory of secondary burial: "wet" phase (putrefaction) → "dry" phase (clean bones) → final ceremony
- Soul in liminal state during "wet" phase; joins ancestors at "dry" phase completion
- Maps directly to slametan: geblag→nyatus = "wet" phase; mendhak→nyewu = bone dissolution
- Nyewu (1000 days) = completion of Hertz's full cycle — body returned to earth

## Gaps and Caveats
1. **No published decomposition study has been conducted in Indonesian volcanic soil** — genuine research gap
2. Closest analogs: Malaysia, Sri Lanka, Queensland, Hawaii
3. The 8× slower rule for burial means surface timelines must be adjusted upward
4. Bone dissolution timeline in acidic soil poorly quantified beyond weeks/months
5. Individual variation (body mass, clothing, coffin, depth) creates wide confidence intervals
6. Traditional Javanese burial is typically shallow (~1m), allowing some soil fauna access
