# E057: Genre Taphonomy — The 5th Layer of Darkness

## Hypothesis
Inscription FORMAT (genre) systematically filters what cultural information
gets recorded. Sanskrit-format inscriptions suppress indigenous vocabulary
and organic material references, making them an information taphonomy filter.

## Method
Classified 268 DHARMA inscriptions by genre (sima, label, dedication, etc.)
using title keywords and word count bins. Compared pre-Indic ratio, organic
mentions, and hyang mentions across genres. Tested genre vs century as
competing explanations for variance in pre-Indic content.

## Results — SUCCESS

### The Genre Filter:
| Genre | N | Pre-Indic% | Organic% | Hyang% | Avg Words |
|-------|---|-----------|----------|--------|-----------|
| Sima (land charter) | 50 | 19.7% | 92.0% | 74.0% | 876 |
| Long format (≥500w) | 33 | 17.9% | 93.9% | 81.8% | 1069 |
| Medium format | 65 | 24.3% | 78.5% | 53.8% | 236 |
| Short format (<100w) | 49 | 18.8% | 53.1% | 28.6% | 47 |
| **Borobudur labels** | **50** | **0.0%** | **0.0%** | **0.0%** | **1** |

### Long vs Short Format:
- **Pre-Indic ratio:** Long 0.197 vs Short 0.087 (Mann-Whitney p<0.000001)
- **Organic mentions:** Long 95.2% vs Short 29.6% (Mann-Whitney p<0.000001)
- **Hyang mentions:** Long 85.7% vs Short 13.0%

### Genre vs Century:
- Century effect: H=62.37, p<0.0001
- Genre effect: H=52.02, p<0.0001
- Both are powerful, but genre is an independent predictor

### The Visibility Window (C8→C9-10):
- Pre-Indic: **+14.4 percentage points** (0.5% → 14.9%)
- Organic: **+63.9 percentage points** (12.7% → 76.6%)
- Mechanism: shift from Sanskrit labels (48 Borobudur) to OJ sima format

### Borobudur = Maximum Darkness:
- 50 labels, avg 1 word, 100% Sanskrit
- 0% pre-Indic, 0% organic, 0% hyang
- These 48 C8 labels make the 8th century look completely Indianized
- Remove them → C8 has only 7 inscriptions (still dark, but much less)

## Interpretation
Genre taphonomy (L5) is distinct from the other 5 layers:
- L1 DESTROYS evidence, L2 SUBMERGES it, L3 MISINTERPRETS it,
  L4 REPLACES it, but L5 FILTERS what gets recorded at all
- The indigenous civilization was there during C8 — the Sanskrit
  inscription format simply refused to record it
- When the format shifted (C9+ OJ sima), the same elements became visible
- This is the most intellectually novel layer: information taphonomy
