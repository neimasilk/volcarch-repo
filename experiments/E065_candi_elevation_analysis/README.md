# E065: Candi Spatial Analysis — Volcanic Proximity Zones

## Purpose
Extend E031's candi analysis with zone classification and distance statistics
to test whether temple builders selected specific volcanic proximity zones.

## Method
Used E031's 142 candi-volcano pairs. Classified by:
1. Distance distribution vs random 2D placement (KS test)
2. Azimuthal distribution (Rayleigh test, quadrant chi²)
3. Distance × quadrant interaction
4. Penanggungan anomaly (50% of all candi)
5. Zone classification: A (<10km), B (10-30km), C (>30km)

## Results

### Key Finding: Zone A Overrepresentation by 17.9×
| Zone | Observed | Expected (random) | Ratio |
|------|----------|-------------------|-------|
| Zone A (<10km) | 60 (42.3%) | 3.4 | **17.9×** |
| Zone B (10-30km) | 65 (45.8%) | 27.0 | 2.4× |
| Zone C (>30km) | 17 (12.0%) | 111.6 | 0.2× |

Chi-squared p < 0.000001. Builders massively preferred volcanic proximity.

### Directional Clustering
- Mean azimuth: **279°** (WEST)
- Rayleigh p < 0.000001 (highly significant)
- West quadrant: 67/142 = **47.2%** (expected 25%)
- East quadrant: 5/142 = **3.5%** (dramatically underrepresented)
- Arjuno-Welirang exception: 16/17 candi to SOUTH (topographic constraint?)

### Penanggungan Sacred Mountain
- 73/142 (51%) of all candi cluster around ONE mountain
- 46/73 (63%) are on the WESTERN side (binomial p < 0.000001)
- Non-Penanggungan candi also show directional clustering (Rayleigh p = 0.0009)

### Distance × Quadrant Interaction
- Western candi are CLOSER (mean 13.6 km, median 6.7 km)
- Southern candi are FARTHER (mean 25.9 km, median 24.5 km)
- Difference not significant (MW p = 0.108) — trend only

## Interpretation
1. **Fertility > Safety**: Builders chose high-risk Zone A (17.9× overrepresentation)
2. **Tephra-aware siting**: Western clustering = windward side avoids ashfall
3. **Sacred geography**: Penanggungan concentration = mountain-as-axis-mundi
4. **Dual rationality**: Practical (west = safe) + cosmological (mountain = sacred)
5. **Strongly supports P11 "Volcanic Informedness"**: Builders were empirically volcano-aware
