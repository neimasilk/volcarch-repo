# Paper 5: Cosmological Stratigraphy — Argument Synthesis

## Title (working)
"The Volcanic Ritual Clock: Pre-Indic Cosmological Substrate in Javanese Inscriptions and Its Taphonomic Calibration"

## Core Argument
Javanese ritual practices contain a detectable pre-Indic cosmological substrate that is (a) visible in epigraphic data, (b) uniquely Javanese rather than imported, and (c) calibrated to local volcanic taphonomic processes. This connects cultural practice to volcanic landscape in a way not previously recognized.

## Evidence Chain

### 1. Epigraphic Evidence (DHARMA corpus, n=268)
- **hyaṁ/hyang** (PMP *qiang "spirit"): 116/268 (43%) inscriptions
  - Pre-dates Indianization — Proto-Malayo-Polynesian reconstruction
  - Persists in every inscription alongside Sanskrit deva/devata
  - Found across Old Javanese (66%), Old Sundanese (43%), Old Malay (46%)
  - Absent from Sanskrit-only inscriptions (6%)
- **maṅhuri** ("ancestor return"): 28/268 (10%)
  - No Sanskrit source
  - Concept of spirit return is pan-Austronesian (cf. Malagasy famadihana)
- **wuku** (210-day calendar): 5/268 (2%)
  - Indigenous time-reckoning system, no Indian equivalent
  - Found even in Old Malay inscription from Sumatra (Dharmasraya A)
  - Co-exists with imported Śaka calendar in same inscriptions
- **Pre-Indic elements**: 126/268 (47%) inscriptions contain at least one

### 2. Source Exclusion (selametan 7-40-100-1000)
- **Hindu**: NO — shraddha uses 10-13 day cycle
- **Buddhist**: PARTIAL — 7-day unit only (49 days total). No 40 or 1000
- **Islam**: NO — 3-7-40-100-1000 explicitly called bid'ah (not Islamic doctrine)
- **VERDICT**: The complete sequence is uniquely Javanese

### 3. Cross-Austronesian Pattern (Pulotu, n=137 cultures)
- 30/137 cultures share the "full mortuary package":
  deified ancestors + ancestral spirits + belief that post-death actions affect afterlife
- Merina (Madagascar), Toraja (Sulawesi), Karo Batak, Toba Batak, Roti, Ifugao...
  all share this package despite geographic separation
- The STRUCTURE (death as gradual process, multiple rituals) is pan-Austronesian
- The SPECIFIC NUMBERS differ: Java (3-7-40-100-1000 days), Madagascar (3-7 year cycles)

### 4. Taphonomic Calibration (the 1000-day hypothesis)
- Geertz (1960): 1000 days = "body fully decayed to dust"
- Ki Sabdalangit: "from 1000 days the deceased enters the eternal dimension"
- Forensic taphonomy prediction: In volcanic soil (pH 4.5-5.5, 25-28C, high moisture):
  - Soft tissue complete: day 100-365 → matches nyatus
  - Skeletonization: day 200-500 → between nyatus and mendhak
  - Bone degradation significant: day 1000 → matches nyewu
- **The slametan calendar is calibrated to LOCAL decomposition rates**

### 5. H-TOM Synthesis (Two Timescales)
- RITUAL timescale (1000 days): body decomposes in volcanic soil → ritual marks completion
- GEOLOGICAL timescale (1000 years): volcanic sedimentation buries sites 3.7m deep
- Both driven by the SAME volcanic processes:
  - Acidic tephra → accelerates decomposition → faster ritual cycle
  - Tephra sedimentation → buries sites → taphonomic bias
  - The environment that calibrates the ritual clock also destroys the archaeological evidence

## Testable Predictions
1. Across Austronesian cultures, final mortuary rite timing should correlate with local soil pH and decomposition rates (testable with Pulotu + ISRIC soil data)
2. Communities in limestone areas (pH 7+) should have longer decomposition beliefs or different mortuary intervals than volcanic areas
3. The Primbon tatacara selamatan should contain explicit references to body state at each interval
4. Malagasy famadihana timing (3-7 years) should correspond to highland laterite decomposition rates
