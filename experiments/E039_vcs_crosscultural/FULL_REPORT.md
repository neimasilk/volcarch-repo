## 1. Research Question

Does living on a volcanic island produce measurably higher ritual complexity in Austronesian cultures? If Volcanic Cultural Selection (VCS) is real, cultures subjected to greater volcanic pressure should show:
- Higher overall ritual complexity
- More elaborate mortuary rituals (ancestor belief, post-death action efficacy)
- Greater investment in costly sacrifices (communal resource pooling)
- Stronger supernatural punishment beliefs (social enforcement of communal norms)

## 2. Data

## 4. Why the Test Failed (and Why It's Useful)

### 4.1 The Classification Problem

Q32 (island type) classifies by **geological formation**, not by **proximity to active volcanoes**:
- "Volcanic high island" = small islands formed by a single volcano (Hawaii, Tanna, Chuuk)
- "Mainland/continent" = large landmasses including Java, Sumatra, Sulawesi — which ARE volcanic but geologically complex

This means the most volcanically active region on Earth (Java, 45 active volcanoes) is classified as "mainland" alongside Australia and mainland Asia.

### 4.2 The Scale Problem

VCS, if real, operates at **local** scale:
- E031: Candi siting shows volcanic awareness within **10-50 km of specific volcanoes** on Java
- E032: Pranata Mangsa encodes seasonality relevant to **monsoon-eruption coupling** in Java specifically
- These are not pan-Austronesian effects — they are Java/Sulawesi/Bali-specific adaptations

A global "volcanic island vs non-volcanic island" test is too coarse. Small volcanic islands in Melanesia (Goodenough, Wogeo, South Pentecost) have few eruptions and small populations — volcanism is present but not the dominant selection pressure.

### 4.3 The Confound

Non-volcanic (mainland) cultures had MORE pre-Austronesian contact (Q22: 1.378 vs 1.000, p=0.028). Pre-Austronesian contact introduces:
- Syncretic religious elaboration
- Additional layers of belief/practice from non-Austronesian populations
- Higher coded ritual complexity scores

This means the non-volcanic group's higher scores may reflect **contact-driven complexity**, not absence of volcanic selection.

### 4.4 The One Signal: Mana (Q21)

Q21 (Mana as spiritual/religious concept) is the only variable where volcanic cultures score higher:
- Volcanic: 0.446 vs Non-volcanic: 0.232 (p=0.006)

This is intriguing because:
- Mana = spiritual power/energy — could encode volcanic "power" concept
- Volcanic landscapes produce visible, dramatic demonstrations of earth's power
- But: doesn't survive Bonferroni, and mana is also associated with Polynesian oceanic cultures

**Status:** Intriguing signal, worth noting, but not evidence.

## 5. What This Means for P11

### DO NOT claim:
- "Volcanic cultures have higher ritual complexity globally"
- "Living on a volcanic island produces more elaborate ritual"
- "Malagasy control confirms VCS" (it doesn't)

### DO claim:
- "VCS is a **local** phenomenon operating at the scale of specific volcanic complexes"
- "E031 (candi siting) and E032 (Pranata Mangsa) provide evidence at the appropriate spatial scale"
- "Global cross-cultural comparison reveals a classification problem: the most volcanically affected cultures (Toraja, Batak, Javanese) are invisible to island-type classification"
- "The negative global result constrains P11's claims to Java/Sulawesi/Bali, preventing over-generalization"

### Reframe P11:
VCS is not "volcanic island → more ritual" (rejected by E039).
VCS is "proximity to specific high-frequency volcanic systems → specific adaptive practices (timing, siting, communal pooling)" — testable only at local scale with GVP eruption frequency data.
