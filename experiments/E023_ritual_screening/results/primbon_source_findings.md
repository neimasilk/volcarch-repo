## KEY FINDINGS

### A. Pre-Hindu-Buddhist Origin — CONFIRMED
From Sholihah et al. (ICCL 2023), citing **Aizid (2015:149)**:
> "Asal-usul selamatan kematian **ada sebelum agama Hindu-Budha datang ke Indonesia**, setelah kedatangan ajaran Hindu-Budha tradisi selamatan kematian memiliki perubahan pada bagian mantra atau doanya dan setelah kedatangan Islam mantra atau doa mengalami penyesuaian yang cukup signifikan yaitu mengganti mantra atau doa sesuai dengan ajaran Islam."

**Translation:** "The origin of death selamatan **existed before Hinduism-Buddhism came to Indonesia**; after the arrival of Hindu-Buddhist teachings, the death selamatan tradition changed in its mantras/prayers, and after the arrival of Islam, the mantras/prayers underwent significant adjustment — replacing them with prayers according to Islamic teachings."

**Implication:** The STRUCTURE and TIMING of the selamatan pre-dates all imported religions. Only the verbal content (mantras → doa) was replaced. The intervals (3-7-40-100-1000) are the indigenous substrate.

### B. Explicit Body Decomposition Reference — SMOKING GUN

From Sholihah et al. (ICCL 2023), page 370, **Mendhak Pindo section**:

> "Tahlilan *mendhak pindho* ini, **jenazah sudah hampir luluh, yang tersisa hanya tulang saja**."

**Translation:** "At the mendhak pindo [2-year] ceremony, **the body has almost dissolved, only bones remain**."

> "Selamatan mendhak mindho ini diadakan dengan bermaksudkan untuk **menyempurnakan semua kulit, darah, dan semacamnya dalam tubuh** orang yang sudah meninggal tersebut."

**Translation:** "The mendhak pindo selamatan is held with the intention of **perfecting all skin, blood, and the like in the body** of the deceased."

**Implication:** The selamatan tradition EXPLICITLY recognizes that the 2-year mark corresponds to near-complete soft tissue decomposition with only bones remaining. The ritual "perfects" (menyempurnakan) the decomposition process — i.e., the ritual marks the taphonomic stage.

### C. Soul Journey ↔ Decomposition Mapping

From the same paper, a clear soul-journey narrative emerges that parallels decomposition:

| Day | Javanese Name | Soul Journey (Sholihah et al.) | Taphonomic Parallel |
|-----|--------------|-------------------------------|---------------------|
| 0 | Geblag | Burial; spirit honored | Fresh stage |
| 3 | Nelung dina | "Roh masih terdapat di dalam rumah" (spirit still in the house) | End of fresh; early bloat |
| 7 | Mitung dina | Family reads Quran juz 1-30 (originally: mantras) | Bloat stage |
| 40 | Matang puluh | "Arwah akan berjalan keluar menuju sang pencipta" (spirit walks out toward Creator) | Soft tissue largely gone |
| 100 | Nyatus dina | "Penyempurnaan ritual; roh masih kembali" (perfecting ritual; spirit still returns) | Skeletonization begins |
| 365 | Mendhak pisan | "Mengingat-ngingat jasa" (remembrance of service) | Full skeletonization |
| 730 | Mendhak pindo | **"Jenazah sudah hampir luluh, yang tersisa hanya tulang saja"** (body almost dissolved, only bones remain) | Bone degradation in acidic soil |
| 1000 | Nyewu | "Puncak rangkaian selamatan" (peak of the selamatan series); grave marker formalized | **"Body fully decayed to dust" (Geertz)** |

### D. Calculation System (Neptu/Pasaran)

The intervals are calculated using Javanese calendrical mathematics, not Gregorian counting:
- System uses 7-day week × 5-day pasaran cycle = 35-day weton cycle
- Each interval has a formula: e.g., nyewu = "nemsarma" (6th day, 5th pasaran)
- 999 ÷ 35 = 28 cycles + 19 remainder
- This means the actual calendar date of nyewu varies — it's the WETON that matters, not the Gregorian day count
- **Implication:** The system is indigenous Javanese mathematics, not imported

### E. Animism-Dynamism Origin — Confirmed

From conclusion (p. 372):
> "Adanya tradisi ini karena dulunya terdapat unsur **animisme dan dinamisme** dalam lingkungan masyarakat, terutama dalam realitas kebudayaan."

**Translation:** "This tradition exists because formerly there were elements of **animism and dynamism** in the community environment, especially in cultural reality."

Also from p. 362:
> "Tradisi selamatan kematian dikenal oleh masyarakat Jawa saat mereka menganut ajaran **animisme dan dinamisme**."

**Translation:** "The death selamatan tradition was known by Javanese society when they followed **animist and dynamist** teachings."

### Kasampurnan (Perfection/Completion) Concept
The Primbon organizing principle for selamatan is **kasampurnan** — each interval marks the "perfection" (completion of decomposition) of specific body components:
- 3 days: nafsu (desires/vital forces from 4 elements: earth, fire, water, wind)
- 7 days: kulit dan rambut (skin and hair)
- 40 days: darah, daging, sumsum, tulang, otot (blood, flesh, marrow, bones, muscles)
- 100 days: badan/jasad (body as a whole)
- 1000 days: "kesempurnaan jasad manusia, termasuk bau dan rasanya — jasad menyatu sepenuhnya dengan tanah" (perfection of human body, including its smell and taste — body fully merged with earth)

### E. Body-Specific Language at Each Interval — CRITICAL FOR P5

From page 437 — **Matangpuluh dina (40 days):**
> "Jasad yang hams disempurnakan adalah berupa **darah, daging, sungsum, jeroan (isi perut), kuku, rambut, tulang, dan otot**."
> Translation: "The body that must be 'perfected' consists of **blood, flesh, marrow, innards, nails, hair, bones, and muscle**."

The 40-day selamatan explicitly names the body components being "perfected" (menyempurnakan) — i.e., the ritual marks the decomposition of EACH tissue type. This is not metaphorical — it's a systematic inventory of what decomposes.

From page 437 — **Nyatus Nyewu dina (100 days):**
> "Tradisi selamatan Nyatus Nyewu dina dimaksudkan untuk menyempumakan semua hal yang bersifat **badan wadhag**."
> Translation: "The selamatan is intended to perfect everything pertaining to the **physical body (badan wadhag)**."

"Badan wadhag" (physical/corporeal body) vs "roh" (spirit) — the selamatan explicitly distinguishes between physical decomposition and spiritual journey. The 100-day mark is about perfecting the PHYSICAL body.

From page 439 — **Mendhak pindho (2 years) — CONFIRMED IN SECOND SOURCE:**
> "Selamatan mendhak pindho dimaksudkan untuk menyempurnakan semua kulit, darah dan semacamnya. Pada saat ini **jenazah sudah hampir luluh, tinggal tulang saja**."
> Translation: "At this time **the body has almost dissolved, only bones remain**."

SAME passage as ICCL paper — independently confirmed across two sources.

### F. Pre-Islamic Origin — Multiple Confirmations

From page 439:
> "Tradisi kematian sangat mungkin merupakan hasil **akumulasi kepercayaan masyarakat Jawa** dengan kepercayaan lain, seperti adanya pengaruh Hindu, Buda, dan Islam."
> Translation: "The death tradition is very possibly the result of the **accumulation of Javanese beliefs** with other beliefs, such as Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic influence."

From page 440:
> "Unsur-unsur **animisme-dinamisme** hingga kini pengaruhnya masih mewarnai sendi-sendi kehidupan mayarakat, terutama dalam ritualitas kebudayaan."
> Translation: "Elements of **animism-dynamism** still color community life to this day, especially in cultural rituality."

From page 441 — **THE KEY PASSAGE:**
> "Dalam kepercayaan lama, upacara dilakukan dengan mengadakan sesaji yang disajikan kepada daya-daya kekuatan gaib (roh-roh, mahluk halus, dewa-dewa) tertentu. Secara luwes **Islam memberikan warna baru pada upacara-upacara itu** dengan sebutan kenduren atau selamatan."
> Translation: "In the old belief, the ceremony was performed by making offerings to supernatural forces (spirits, subtle beings, gods). **Islam flexibly gave new color to those ceremonies** with the designation kenduren or selamatan."

**Implication:** The ceremonies EXISTED before Islam. Islam merely "recolored" (memberikan warna baru) them — changing the prayers/mantras but preserving the structure and timing.
